# Market & Positioning Research — Temporai Solutions (tempor.ai)
*Research date: 2026-07-08. Sources prioritized 2025–2026.*

## 1. Positioning landscape: AI dev shops / agent studios

**Real examples of positioning language & offer structures:**

- **Fractional AI** (fractional.ai) — "AI Transformation, Powered by Engineering Excellence"; "real impact, not just flashy demos"; "elite San Francisco engineering team"; Anthropic-backed; case-study-led ("reducing hallucinations by over 80%" for Zapier). Serves enterprises + private equity. [fractional.ai](https://www.fractional.ai/)
- **AE Studio** (ae.studio) — ~150-person bootstrapped applied-AI studio; differentiates on *reliability*: evals, red-teaming, guardrails, observability — plus frontier alignment research (DARPA, Anthropic). [ae.studio](https://ae.studio/)
- **Sprint Studio** (sprintstudio.co) — solo-founder shop; "We build AI agents that actually work"; two-tier offer: **AI Discovery Sprint** (3–5 days, roadmap + working prototype) → **Custom AI Agent** (4–8 weeks, production deploy + 30 days support); pricing "on request". [sprintstudio.co](https://www.sprintstudio.co/)
- **Active Logic** — "agentic custom software" incl. MCP servers, "senior U.S.-based engineers, director-led governance, production-grade reliability". [activelogic.com](https://activelogic.com/mcp-server-development)
- **Opcito** — productized "MCP Server Development Services": custom MCP servers for existing products "to enable easy consumption in the agentic AI world". [opcito.com](https://www.opcito.com/mcp-server-development)
- **Neurons Lab** — vertical AI-agent development, listed among top agent dev companies 2026. [neurons-lab.com](https://neurons-lab.com/articles/ai-agent-development-services/)

**Engagement/pricing norms (2025–26):** discovery sprints (3–5 days) → fixed-scope builds ($10K–$500K+) → retainers ($2K–$50K/mo tiers); outcome-based pricing is the hot frontier — "metering results the buyer actually wants rather than inputs" — but mostly for repeatable agent products, not bespoke builds. [Digital Agency Network](https://digitalagencynetwork.com/ai-agency-pricing/), [Growth Unhinged](https://www.growthunhinged.com/p/ai-agent-pricing-framework), [Chargebee](https://www.chargebee.com/blog/pricing-ai-agents-playbook/)

**What's commodity:** "we build AI agents for you," and increasingly even "production-grade, not demos" — Sprint Studio, Fractional AI, and Active Logic all lead with an anti-demo claim. The real gap it points at is genuine: only ~14% of orgs have deploy-ready agentic solutions and ~11% run them in production despite 30% exploring / 38% piloting. [Alvarez & Marsal](https://www.alvarezandmarsal.com/thought-leadership/demystifying-ai-agents-in-2025-separating-hype-from-reality-and-navigating-market-outlook), [IBM](https://www.ibm.com/think/insights/ai-agents-2025-expectations-vs-reality). Differentiation now comes from domain depth, proprietary-data leverage, and *proof* of production operation — not the claim itself.

## 2. AI as investor signal

- **Premium is real:** seed-stage AI startups command ~42% higher valuations than non-AI peers; AI took ~33% of total VC funding in 2026; AI VC hit a record $256B in 2025 (~3× 2024). [Eqvista](https://eqvista.com/ai-startup-fundraising-trends/), [Qubit Capital](https://qubit.capital/blog/ai-startup-fundraising-trends), [Crunchbase](https://news.crunchbase.com/venture/funding-surges-all-stages-ai-north-america-q1-2026/)
- **But AI-washing skepticism is priced in:** "thin wrappers on public LLMs are no longer fundable"; diligence now demands unique architecture, proprietary data pipelines, paying pilots, active usage/retention evidence — customer references contradicting the pitch are the most common late-stage deal killer. [Qubit due-diligence checklist](https://qubit.capital/blog/ai-startup-due-diligence-documents-metrics), [Waveup](https://waveup.com/blog/how-to-raise-money-for-ai-startup/)
- **Governance is now diligence surface:** investors assess AI governance/audit systems (e.g., ISO/IEC 42001) and it "directly affects valuations." [PrometAI](https://prometai.app/blog/ai-regulatory-trends-2025-impact-on-startup-fundraising-growth)
- **Crypto × AI specifically:** AI×crypto is the most-discussed thematic (decentralized compute, on-chain agent infra), *but* "the narrative-only window has closed — fundamentals win meetings," and VCs explicitly flag decorative integrations ("AI agents on blockchains primarily as marketing positioning"). Many crypto VCs quietly pivoted to AI. [VaaSBlock](https://www.vaasblock.com/news/crypto-venture-capital-funding-cycle-infrastructure-2026/)

**Implication:** "credible AI capability is a fundraising signal" is well supported — but only *diligence-survivable* AI (real usage, real architecture, auditability) earns the premium. That is exactly what a web3 team can buy from a shop that ships verifiable, production AI.

## 3. MCP / A2A agent-readiness momentum

- **MCP is the de-facto standard:** OpenAI adopted it (Mar 2025), Google/Microsoft followed (Apr–May 2025); Anthropic donated MCP to the Linux Foundation's Agentic AI Foundation in Dec 2025 (backed by AWS, Google, Microsoft, OpenAI, Bloomberg, Cloudflare). 10,000+ public MCP servers; registry showed ~9,650 servers by May 2026; one July-2026 analysis puts 78% of enterprise AI teams with MCP-backed agents in production. [Digital Applied](https://www.digitalapplied.com/blog/mcp-adoption-statistics-2026-model-context-protocol), [WorkOS](https://workos.com/blog/everything-your-team-needs-to-know-about-mcp-in-2026), [andrew.ooo](https://andrew.ooo/answers/mcp-model-context-protocol-enterprise-adoption-july-2026/)
- **Named shippers:** Linear, Notion, Atlassian, Canva, MongoDB, Airtable, Monday.com, Zapier; Adobe Marketo (Apr 2026, 100+ operations), Klaviyo, Brevo, Knak. [tooldirectory.ai](https://tooldirectory.ai/blog/state-of-mcp-servers-2026), [Knak](https://knak.com/blog/mcp-adoption-in-2026-what-marketers-need-to-know/)
- **A2A:** launched Apr 2025 (50+ partners), Linux Foundation governance since Jun 2025; 150+ supporting orgs at its one-year mark with native integration in Azure AI Foundry, Amazon Bedrock AgentCore, Google Cloud. [Linux Foundation](https://www.linuxfoundation.org/press/a2a-protocol-surpasses-150-organizations-lands-in-major-cloud-platforms-and-sees-enterprise-production-use-in-first-year)
- **"Agent as your second customer" discourse is mainstream:** e.g., agentsfirst.dev — "Every product is getting a second customer — the human who pays, and the agent who decides"; PostHog's "golden rules of agent-first product engineering." [agentsfirst.dev](https://agentsfirst.dev/), [PostHog](https://newsletter.posthog.com/p/the-golden-rules-of-agent-first-product)
- **"MCP-enablement as a service" is emerging but thin:** Opcito and Active Logic sell it explicitly (typical engagement: API/auth audit → typed tool catalog on existing APIs), but no dominant boutique owns the category yet — it's mostly a line item at generalist shops. [Opcito](https://www.opcito.com/mcp-server-development), [Active Logic](https://activelogic.com/mcp-server-development)

[eco.com](https://eco.com/support/en/articles/14796365-tees-for-ai-agents-verifiable-compute), [Red Hat](https://next.redhat.com/2025/10/23/enhancing-ai-inference-security-with-confidential-computing-a-path-to-private-data-inference-with-proprietary-llms/), [Phemex Academy](https://phemex.com/academy/what-is-verifiable-ai-cryptographic-proofs-blockchain)

## 4. Differentiation recommendations

**Whitespace read:** Big consultancies own enterprise agentic transformation (Accenture: $5.9B GenAI bookings FY2025, revenue tripled — [CIO Dive](https://www.ciodive.com/news/accenture-generative-ai-revenue-skills-training-data-modernization/761161/)) but are slow, expensive, and don't ship verifiable/web3-native AI. Offshore shops compete on price with demo-ware. Boutique studios claim "production-grade" but almost none can *prove* it cryptographically or speak crypto natively.

**How Temporai should position:**

1. **Own the intersection, not a category.** "Verifiable, production AI for teams that will be diligenced" — web3 founders raising, and SMEs facing AI-governance scrutiny. Nobody credible owns "AI your investors can verify."
2. **Make the delivery model the proof.** One senior builder orchestrating agents *is* the product demo — "we run on what we sell." Live prod systems (DeFi copilot, 0Gora) beat any case-study PDF.
3. **Claim MCP/A2A enablement as a named productized offer** (audit → typed tool surface → shipped server) before the category consolidates; it's real demand with no boutique owner yet.
4. **Lead with guardrails + auditability as features, not disclaimers** — AE Studio validates this lane; TEE-verified inference is a step beyond anything boutiques offer.
5. **Sprint-shaped front door, retainer back end** — matches market norms (3–5 day discovery → fixed build → $2–15K/mo retainer) and de-risks the solo-shop objection.
6. **Sell to the fundraise:** for web3 clients, frame deliverables as diligence artifacts (real usage metrics, attestations, audit trails) — the 42%-premium / AI-washing-skepticism pincer makes this concrete ROI.

**Tagline/phrase patterns:**
- "AI you can prove. Shipped in weeks."
- "Your next user is an agent. Your next diligence is on your AI. Be ready for both."
- "We don't replace your team — we hand them the leverage we use ourselves."
- "Verifiable by design. Production by default."
- "One senior builder. A fleet of agents. Zero demo-ware."

**Pitfalls to avoid:**
- "Production-grade, not demos" as the *headline* — it's already every boutique's line; prove it instead (live URLs, attestation links).
- Web3 AI-washing tropes ("AI agents on the blockchain") — VCs now explicitly discount decorative crypto×AI. Lead with the verification problem it solves.
- Overclaiming scale (24/7 team, enterprise SLAs) or outcome-based pricing without measurement infrastructure — hollow for a one-senior-builder shop and easily punctured in a reference call.
